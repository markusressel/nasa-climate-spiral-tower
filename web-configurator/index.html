Anomalies Explained

Values represent the deviation from the long-term average temperature. A value of +1.0°C means the temperature was 1.0 degree Celsius warmer than the 1951-1980 average.

Early records (1880-1920) exhibit cold anomalies (blue, shrinking radius), while recent years display escalating warm anomalies (red, expanding radius).
